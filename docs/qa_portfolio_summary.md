# Audio QA Evaluation — Portfolio Samples

This set of samples demonstrates structured audio evaluation across varying levels of degradation, focusing on intelligibility, artifact detection, and commercial usability.

## Summary

| Sample | Condition | Severity | Key Issue | Outcome |
|--------|----------|----------|-----------|--------|
| 01 | Heavy Noise | High | Noise masking speech | Unusable |
| 02 | Moderate Noise + Distortion | Medium | Transient distortion | Borderline |
| 03 | Moderate Noise + Tonal/Compression | Low–Medium | Harshness + compression | Usable |

## Key Observations

- Noise impacts both intelligibility and system reliability  
- Transient distortion affects clarity even when speech remains intelligible  
- Tonal imbalance and compression artifacts can degrade quality without breaking usability  

## Evaluation Approach

Each sample was evaluated using:
- Perceptual listening (first-pass impression)
- Structured issue identification
- Artifact tagging (NOI, DST, CMP, HAR, etc.)
- Quantitative scoring (Naturalness, Clarity, Prosody, Artifact Severity, Commercial Readiness)
- Cause analysis

## Takeaway

Effective audio QA requires not just identifying artifacts, but understanding how they impact usability across different conditions. This includes distinguishing between complete failure cases and audio that remains functionally usable despite degradation.
